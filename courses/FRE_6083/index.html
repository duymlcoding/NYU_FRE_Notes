FRE 6083: Quantitative Methods#

Welcome to the course notes for FRE 6083: Quantitative Methods taught by Professor Agnès Tourin.

About This Course#

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to quantitative methods used in financial engineering and risk management. The material covers probability theory, stochastic processes, and their applications to derivative pricing and portfolio optimization.

About These Notes#

These notes are designed to accompany lectures and provide:

  • Comprehensive coverage of course topics

  • Mathematical derivations and proofs

  • Worked examples with step-by-step solutions

  • Physical interpretations of key formulas

  • Practice problems and exercises

Course Topics#

The course is organized into 14 lectures covering:

Probability Foundations (Lectures 1-3)#

  • Sequences and sums of random variables

  • Convergence concepts and limit theorems

  • Markov chains and their applications

Stochastic Processes (Lectures 4-7)#

  • The Poisson process

  • Binomial asset pricing model

  • Introduction to stochastic processes

  • Brownian motion

Advanced Topics (Lectures 9-14)#

  • Stochastic calculus and Ito’s formula

  • Black-Scholes model and PDE

  • Finite difference methods

  • Portfolio optimization

  • American options and Greeks
