# FRE 6103: Valuation Methods for Financial Engineering

Welcome to the course notes for **FRE 6103: Valuation Methods for Financial Engineering** based on the work of David C. Shimko.

## About This Course

This course provides a comprehensive foundation in valuation methods essential for financial engineering. The material covers fundamental concepts of time value of money, benchmarking, simulation techniques, and the General Valuation Equation (GVE) as applied to various asset classes.

## About These Notes

These notes are designed to provide:
- Comprehensive coverage of valuation fundamentals
- Step-by-step derivations and proofs
- Real-world applications in financial engineering
- Practice problems with detailed solutions
- Clear explanations of complex mathematical concepts

## Course Topics

The course is organized into 9 chapters covering:

### Foundations (Chapters 1-3)
- Valuation methods and the General Valuation Equation
- Annuities, perpetuities, and time value of money
- Complications in cash flow valuation and their resolutions

### Fixed Income (Chapter 4)
- Bond pricing and yield calculations
- Duration, convexity, and interest rate risk
- Term structure of interest rates

### Quantitative Methods (Chapters 5-6)
- Monte Carlo simulation techniques
- Stochastic processes in finance
- Modeling uncertainty and randomness

### Asset Pricing Models (Chapters 7-9)
- Risk, capital requirements, and valuation
- Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)
- Cash Flow Pricing Model (CFPM)
- Derivative pricing in static frameworks
