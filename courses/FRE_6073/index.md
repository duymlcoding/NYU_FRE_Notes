# FRE 6073: Introduction to Derivatives

## Course Information

**Course Title:** Introduction to Derivatives
**Course Code:** FRE 6073
**Instructor:** Based on John C. Hull's "Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives" (11th Edition)
**Program:** NYU Tandon Financial Risk Engineering

---

## Welcome

Welcome to the FRE 6073 course notes! These comprehensive study materials cover the fundamental principles of derivative securities, from basic concepts to advanced pricing models and risk management techniques.

## Course Content

### Part I: Foundations

**Chapter 1: Introduction to Derivatives**
- What are derivatives?
- Market structure and trading venues
- Uses of derivatives: hedging, speculation, arbitrage

**Chapter 2: Forward and Futures Contracts**
- Forward contracts: mechanics and terminology
- Futures contracts: exchanges and specifications
- Margins and daily settlement
- Delivery mechanisms

**Chapter 3: Pricing and Valuation**
- Forward pricing formulas
- Futures vs. forward prices
- Stock indices, currencies, and commodities
- Cost of carry and convenience yield

### Part II: Market Infrastructure

**Chapter 4: Interest Rates and Repo Markets**
- Treasury markets and the risk-free rate
- LIBOR phase-out and new reference rates (SOFR)
- Repo markets: tri-party, bilateral, GCF
- Compounding conventions

**Chapter 5: Bonds and Zero Rates**
- Bootstrap method for zero curve
- Bond pricing and yields
- Par yields
- Duration and convexity

### Part III: Pricing Theory

**Chapter 6: Binomial Asset Pricing Model**
- One-period binomial model
- Risk-neutral valuation
- Multi-period trees
- Choosing u and d parameters

**Chapter 7: Stochastic Processes and Brownian Motion**
- Markov processes
- Wiener processes (Brownian motion)
- Generalized Wiener processes
- Itô processes and Itô's lemma
- Lognormal distribution of stock prices

**Chapter 8: Black-Scholes-Merton Model**
- Derivation of the BSM differential equation
- BSM formulas for European options
- Risk-neutral valuation interpretation
- Applications and extensions

### Part IV: Advanced Topics

**Chapter 9: Option Greeks and Risk Management**
- Delta: sensitivity to underlying price
- Gamma: curvature and delta hedging
- Theta: time decay
- Vega: volatility sensitivity
- Rho: interest rate sensitivity
- Portfolio Greek management

**Chapter 10: Volatility Smiles and Trading Strategies**
- Volatility smiles for currencies and equities
- Implied distributions
- Volatility surfaces
- Principal protected notes
- Spreads and combinations
