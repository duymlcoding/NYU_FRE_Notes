WHAT IS A DERIVATIVE?
A derivative is an instrument whose value depends on, or is derived 
from, the value of another asset.
Examples: futures, forwards, swaps, options
1

WHY DERIVATIVES ARE IMPORTANT
Derivatives play a key role in transferring risks in the economy
The underlying assets include stocks, currencies, interest rates, 
commodities, debt instruments, electricity prices, insurance 
payouts, the weather
Many financial transactions have embedded derivatives
2

HOW DERIVATIVES ARE TRADED
On exchanges such as the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME)
In the over -the-counter (OTC) market where traders working for banks, 
fund managers and corporate treasurers contact each other directly
3

THE OTC MARKET
Largely unregulated prior to 2008
Banks acted as market makers quoting bids and asks
Master agreements usually defined how transactions between 
two parties would be handled
Some transactions were cleared through central counterparties 
(CCPs). A CCP stands between the two sides to a transaction in 
the same way that an exchange does
4

SINCE 2008
OTC market has become regulated. Objectives:
ÔÇ≠Reduce systemic risk
ÔÇ≠Increase transparency
In the U.S and some other countries, standardized OTC products 
must be traded on swap execution facilities (SEFs) which are 
electronic platforms similar to exchanges
CCPs must be used to clear standardized transactions between 
financial institutions in most countries
All trades must be reported to a central repository
5

HOW DERIVATIVES ARE USED
To hedge risks
To speculate (take a view on the future direction of the market)
To lock in an arbitrage profit
To change the nature of a liability
To change the nature of an investment without incurring the 
costs of selling one portfolio and buying another
6

FORWARD PRICE
The forward price for a contract is the delivery price that would 
be applicable to the contract if were negotiated today (i.e., it is 
the delivery price that would make the contract worth exactly 
zero)
The forward price may be different  for contracts of different 
maturities
7

long position
The party that has agreed to sell has what is termed a 
short position

OPTIONS

OVERNIGHT RATES
Unsecured borrowing and lending between banks as they 
adjust the reserve requirements they are required to 
keep with the central bank
Referred to as the Fed Funds Rate in the U.S. 
The effective fed funds rate is the volume weighted 
median of the rates on brokered transactions
Central bank may intervene with its own transactions to 
raise or lower the overnight rate

REPO
A sale of a security and an agreement to repurchase the security on a 
specified future date at a pre -arranged price. 
The seller of the security is the borrower of the cash and essentially 
obtains a collateralized loan.
The rate of interest is calculated from the difference between the sale 
price and the repurchase price and is the repo interest.
In the event the securities seller is unable to repurchase the securities, 
i.e. repay the loan, the cash provider is entitled to liquidate the 
securities to obtain repayment.
4

SOFR
SOFR is based on transaction data drawn from three segments of the repo 
market:
1) Tri-party Treasury general collateral (GC) repo transactions cleared 
and settled by the Bank of New York Mellon, excluding transactions made 
through the FICC GCF (General Collateral Financing) repo market and 
excluding transactions in which the Fed is a counterparty
2) Tri-party Treasury general collateral (GC) repo transactions made 
through the FICC GCF repo market, for which the FICC acts as central 
counterparty
3) Bilateral Treasury repo transactions cleared through the FICC DVP ( 
Delivery vs. Payment service)
8

GENERAL COLLATERAL
All tri -party repos, and some bilateral repos, are made against a pool of 
general collateral rather than specific securities. In a general collateral (GC) 
repo, the cash provider stipulates a population of acceptable collateral 
(Treasury securities), but does not stipulate the specific securities that the 
securities provider must pledge. 
9

TRI-PARTY REPO
‚ñ™ Uses a ‚Äútri -party‚Äù agent ( a custodian bank or clearing organization) to 
serve as an intermediary between the buyer and the seller. 
‚ñ™ The collateral agent minimizes the operational burden in the transaction, 
prices the securities, holds the collateral separately from the dealer‚Äôs 
assets, layering in protection to the buyer in the event the dealer goes 
bankrupt.
10


FICC GCF REPO MARKET
‚ñ™ GCF Repo permits FICC‚Äôs netting members to trade cash and securities 
among themselves based on negotiated rates and terms. 
‚ñ™ Trades are completed on an anonymous basis through interdealer brokers.
‚ñ™ FICC acts as the central counterparty in a GCF Repo, serving as the legal 
counterparty to each side of the repo transaction.
11

BILATERAL REPO
‚ñ™ Counterparties exchange cash and securities without the use of a third 
party to manage collateral and facilitate centralized settlement. The 
parties may identify specific securities for their custodians to transfer. As a 
result, the bilateral repo market may contain transaction data for securities 
that trade ‚Äúspecial‚Äù ‚Äì i.e. cash providers may be willing to accept a lower 
return in order to secure a particular security.
‚ñ™ FRB NY ranks all FICC DVP bilateral repo trades by their transaction 
rates, from lowest to highest, and then removes 25% of the trading volume 
corresponding to the lowest transaction rates. The goal of this data filtering  
is to remove repo transactions in which treasury collateral is likely to be 
trading ‚Äúspecial‚Äù, so as to achieve a residual set of bilateral repo data 
that largely reflects GC transactions. 
12

BILATERAL REPO 
‚ñ™ Bilateral repo transactions may be cleared or non -cleared
‚ñ™ Cleared : Specific form of tri -party repo in which an approved member 
of the Fixed Income Clearing Corporation (FICC) sponsors a non -dealer 
counterparty to transact on the FICC‚Äôs cleared repo platform. This platform 
settles through the Delivery Versus Payment settlement process, a method 
which permits the transfer of securities only after payment is made. 
‚ñ™ Non-cleared bilateral repo transactions do not settle on the clearing 
banks tri -party repo platforms and this segment is not used in SOFR 
calculations.

VOLUME WEIGHTED  MEDIAN
‚ñ™ FRB NY pools the three sources of data and then ranks the aggregate of 
repo trading volumes by their transaction rates, then computes the volume 
weighted median repo rate.  
‚ñ™ More robust statistic than a volume weighted average.
‚ñ™ The value it produces is an interest rate level that has actually been
